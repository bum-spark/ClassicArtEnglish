The Judgment
of Paris

A myth about beauty and jealousy that started the Trojan War — depicted from ancient ceramics to Baroque masterpieces.

The Myth

What Happened?

At a wedding among the gods, Eris, the goddess of discord, was not invited. Angry, she threw a golden apple inscribed "For the fairest" into the party.

Three goddesses claimed the apple: Hera (queen of the gods), Athena (goddess of wisdom), and Aphrodite (goddess of love and beauty).

Zeus refused to judge. Instead, he sent them to Paris, a young prince of Troy. Each goddess offered him a bribe:

Hera

Offered power — rule over all of Asia

Athena

Offered wisdom — victory in every battle

Aphrodite

Offered love — the most beautiful woman in the world

Paris chose Aphrodite. She led him to Helen of Sparta, the most beautiful mortal woman — who was already married to King Menelaus. Paris took Helen to Troy.

Menelaus called on every Greek king to get her back. This is how the Trojan War began — a ten-year siege that destroyed an entire civilization, all because of a golden apple.

The Insight

"From clay pots to oil canvases,
humans keep retelling the same stories."

The Judgment of Paris has been depicted for over 2,500 years. Each version reflects the technology, values, and aesthetics of its time — but the human emotions remain unchanged.
