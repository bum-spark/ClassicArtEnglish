Classical Era — Rome

Roman Legacy

They conquered Greece with armies, but Greece conquered them with culture. And their combined legacy lives in every part of our daily lives.

The Connection

Rome & Greece

Rome conquered Greece militarily — they had the bigger army. But Greek culture was so impressive that the Romans could not help adopting it as their own.

The similarities between the two cultures are so deep that historians often call it Greco-Roman culture, as if they were one thing. And when we say "Classical Antiquity," we mean both of them together.

Want a clear example? Look at their temples. The Greek Parthenon and Roman temples follow almost the same design: a rectangular base, rows of stone columns with carved capitals, vertical grooves running down each column, and a triangular roof. They are essentially the same concept.

The Parthenon, Athens — Greek, c. 447 BC

The Pantheon, Rome — Roman, c. 125 AD

Same columns. Same grand entrances. Same monumental scale.
Built centuries apart — but clearly the same tradition.

The Gods

Same Gods, Different Names

The Romans took Greek gods and renamed them — same stories, same roles, just different names. Here are the most important ones:

⚡

Zeus → Jupiter

King of the gods, ruler of the sky

💖

Aphrodite → Venus

Goddess of love and beauty

💪

Heracles → Hercules

The greatest hero — son of Zeus

🦉

Athena → Minerva

Goddess of wisdom and strategy

⚔️

Ares → Mars

God of war

🌊

Poseidon → Neptune

God of the sea

The art stayed the same too. Remember the Farnese Hercules? Both the Greek and Roman versions show him holding the Golden Apples of the Hesperides, with the same powerful muscles, the same exhausted pose. Same story, same style — just centuries apart.

Our Inheritance

Rome Is Still With Us

The influence of Greco-Roman culture on our daily lives is enormous — and you probably do not even notice it.

📜

Language

Spanish, French, Italian, Portuguese, and Romanian all come from Latin — the language spoken in Rome. Even English is full of Latin words.

⚖️

Law

Our legal systems — courts, lawyers, written laws — are based on Roman Law, the most influential legal system in history.

The Transition

From Rome to Christianity

In ancient Rome, many religions existed side by side. But one of them started to grow faster and faster: Christianity.

You might picture ancient Rome and Christianity as completely separate things. But Christianity spread through the Roman Empire and eventually became its official religion.

This is why Christian art is deeply inspired by Roman art, which was inspired by Greek art. It is one continuous tradition — one era building on the next, like layers of paint on a canvas.

And this is exactly where our journey continues: the Middle Ages and Christian art.

What We Learned

Classical Era — Recap

🏛️ Greece

  • • Paintings barely survived — but texts tell us they valued hyperrealism
  • • Sculpture evolved from stiff & geometric to incredibly lifelike
  • • Materials: marble, bronze, gold — and color (they were painted!)
  • • Over 50,000 ceramic vases survive from Athens alone

🏟️ Rome

  • • Conquered Greece militarily but absorbed Greek culture
  • • Same gods with new names: Zeus → Jupiter, Aphrodite → Venus
  • • Our language, law, calendar, and planet names come from Rome
  • • Christian art grew directly from Roman art traditions
