Judith &
Holofernes

A biblical tale of courage, painted by masters across five centuries. Each artist reveals something different.

The Story

What Happened?

In the Old Testament, Holofernes was an Assyrian general who was about to destroy the city of Bethulia. The people were terrified.

Judith, a beautiful and courageous widow, came up with a plan. She dressed in her finest clothes and went to Holofernes' camp, pretending she wanted to betray her people.

Holofernes was captivated by her beauty and invited her to a banquet. He drank heavily. When he was drunk and asleep, Judith took his own sword and cut off his head.

She returned to Bethulia with the head. Without their general, the Assyrian army fled. Judith saved her people.

Why This Story?

Artists loved this story because it has everything: beauty, danger, courage, violence, and a surprising hero. But each era painted it differently, revealing what that time valued most.

The Insight

"Same story, different centuries.
Each painting is a mirror of its time."

From Botticelli's grace to Artemisia's rage to Goya's horror — the story stays the same, but what artists choose to show tells us everything about their era.
